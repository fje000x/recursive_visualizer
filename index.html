Why I Built This

When I was grinding LeetCode, I could read solutions but never actually see what they were doing. Why does the call go left first? When does the stack unwind? What does BFS look like on real input?

YouTube wasn't the answer either. Most videos don't draw the data structure, some just read the code, and the good ones are buried. You watch four videos for one problem and still don't get it.

I wanted something where I could pause, step forward, step back, and watch every call frame like a visual debugger built for algorithm problems.

So I built AlgoFlowz. A curated visual walkthrough for the top 150 LeetCode interview questions. Trees, arrays, two pointers, stacks, graphs. Every major pattern. The tool I wish I had when I started.
